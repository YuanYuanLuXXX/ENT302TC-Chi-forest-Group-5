Maintenance & Logistics Standard Operating Procedure

Applicable to all Chi-Forest smart vending deployments on campus (Points A–G). Target uptime ≥ 98%, with integrated cloud monitoring and field operations.
Smart Vending IoT Operations Chrono-Nutrition Campus Deployment

1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the maintenance, replenishment, and logistics workflow for the Chi-Forest Chrono-Nutrition Smart Vending System. It aims to ensure consistent machine uptime, accurate stock levels, reliable recommendation logic, and seamless interaction between digital and physical components, thereby supporting long-term commercial viability and user satisfaction.

2. Scope

This SOP applies to:

  • All IoT-enabled smart vending units (SVUs) running the Chrono-Nutrition Recommendation Engine (CNR).
  • On-campus deployment locations (Interactive Vending Map Points A–G).
  • Field technicians, logistics partners, and digital operations teams managing the system.
  • Core processes including replenishment, cleaning, diagnostics, and incident handling.

3. Definitions

Term Definition
Smart Vending Unit (SVU) IoT-connected vending machine equipped with display, sensors, payment module, and cloud integration.
Chrono-Nutrition Recommendation Engine (CNR) Cloud-based engine that generates time- and context-sensitive beverage recommendations.
Remote Monitoring System (RMS) Backend interface for tracking inventory, machine status, errors, and environmental parameters.
3PL Third-party logistics provider responsible for stock replenishment and basic handling.
Hot Zone Hours Peak traffic periods (09:00–11:00, 14:00–17:00, 20:00–22:00) when vending usage is highest.

4. Roles & Responsibilities

4.1 Operations Team

  • Maintain SVU uptime at or above 98%.
  • Verify the accuracy of product nutritional and rule-based metadata in the CNR engine.
  • Deploy configuration and algorithm updates in coordination with data and analytics.

4.2 Logistics & Replenishment Team (3PL)

  • Perform routine on-site replenishment according to RMS alerts and schedule.
  • Apply FIFO (First-In-First-Out) principles to minimise wastage.
  • Report machine damage, refrigeration issues, or anomalies immediately.

4.3 Technical Support

  • Resolve IoT connectivity and hardware issues.
  • Conduct periodic preventive maintenance and firmware updates.
  • Support critical incident response (e.g., extended outages, safety risks).

4.4 Data & Analytics Team

  • Analyse consumption patterns to optimise SKU mix per location.
  • Review engagement data to refine recommendation logic.
  • Produce quarterly performance and optimisation reports.

5. Logistics SOP

5.1 Replenishment Triggers

Replenishment is initiated when any of the following conditions is met:

  • RMS indicates that stock of any top-5 SKU at a location falls below 30%.
  • Scheduled replenishment days (e.g., Monday and Thursday) occur.
  • Expected surge events (sports games, festivals, exam weeks) are flagged by operations.

5.2 On-Site Replenishment Procedure

Step Action
1. Arrival & Authentication Technician arrives at the SVU, verifies identity via QR or NFC staff credential; RMS logs entry time.
2. Open Machine Unlock with authorised key or card; ensure machine is stable and power is on.
3. Remove Expired/Damaged Stock Remove compromised products, record quantities in RMS, and move them to the waste stream.
4. Load New Stock (FIFO) Place newer items behind older ones; verify product positioning matches planogram.
5. Scan & Confirm Scan barcodes to confirm SKU and nutritional metadata; ensure alignment with backend configuration.
6. Quick Functional Test Test payment, QR scanner, display, product dispense, and refrigeration.
7. Close & Submit Report Securely close the door, lock SVU, and submit a replenishment report via RMS mobile interface.

6. Maintenance SOP

6.1 Daily Remote Monitoring

Operations Team checks the RMS dashboard to review:

  • Online/offline status of each SVU.
  • Temperature readings (target range: 3–6°C).
  • Error logs (payment retries, stuck coils, display errors).
  • Synchronisation status of recommendation data and transactions.

Any anomaly must result in a maintenance ticket being raised within 15 minutes.

6.2 Weekly On-Site Routine

  • Clean external surfaces, especially touchscreen and QR scanner.
  • Check ventilation, fans, and general physical condition.
  • Remove dust from accessible internal components where safe.
  • Capture photos for documentation and upload to RMS.

6.3 Monthly Preventive Maintenance

  • Apply firmware and software updates as scheduled.
  • Deep clean coils and internal refrigeration components.
  • Inspect structural integrity, hinges, and locks.
  • Validate that the CNR engine returns expected recommendation sets.

6.4 Quarterly Full-System Diagnostics

  • Conduct hardware stress tests and network performance evaluations.
  • Review energy consumption and cost efficiency.
  • Analyse SKU-level performance and adjust product mix.
  • Assess recommendation relevance and user engagement metrics.

7. Contingency & Issue Handling

7.1 Machine Offline

  • RMS raises an automatic alert when an SVU is offline for more than 10 minutes.
  • Technician is dispatched within 2 hours for critical locations (e.g., A, C, D).
  • If downtime exceeds 12 hours, escalation to Technical Support is mandatory.

7.2 Payment Failure

  • Display user-facing message suggesting alternative payment options where possible.
  • Log error details (payment provider, timestamp, amount) in RMS.
  • Apply a soft reset of the payment module; if unresolved, disable affected payment type temporarily.

7.3 Product Safety Incidents

  • Immediately block suspicious SKUs in RMS, preventing further purchases.
  • Remove relevant batches on-site and label them for inspection.
  • Report incident to Operations and comply with F&B safety regulations.

7.4 Refrigeration Failure

  • Trigger alert when temperature exceeds 8°C for more than 10 minutes.
  • Disable temperature-sensitive SKUs until issue is resolved.
  • Dispatch technician promptly; if repair is delayed, empty the unit and temporarily shut it down.

8. Health & Safety

  • Technicians must wear gloves and visible identification when servicing machines.
  • Products must not be left outside refrigeration for more than 10–15 minutes, depending on type.
  • Adhere to national F&B hygiene standards for handling packaged beverages.
  • Ensure cable management and access paths around each SVU are safe and unobstructed.

9. Sustainability & Waste Management

  • Prioritise recyclable packaging in SKU selection where commercially feasible.
  • Separate damaged or expired products into appropriate recycling or disposal streams.
  • Track monthly waste volume and identify opportunities for reduction.
  • Coordinate with 3PL providers to optimise delivery routes and reduce carbon footprint.

10. Documentation & Reporting

All actions must be recorded in the RMS, including:

  • Replenishment logs and stock discrepancies.
  • Maintenance tickets, resolutions, and responsible personnel.
  • Incident reports (payment failures, safety events, breakdowns).
  • Preventive maintenance and diagnostic results.

Data should be retained for at least 24 months for audit, performance tracking, and continuous improvement.

11. Review & Updates

This SOP is reviewed every six months by the Operations, Technical Support, and Data & Analytics teams. Revisions are documented with version numbers and communicated to all relevant parties. Updated procedures should be reflected in the RMS operator dashboard and training materials for field staff.
