ğŸ—‚ï¸ Information Architecture

How content is structured across the prototype screens.

Top-level navigation Global IA

The prototype uses a flat top navigation bar to switch sections:

  • Home
  • Health Questionnaire
  • Recommendations
  • My Chi-Forest Profile
  • Points & Challenges
  • About the Project
Low cognitive load Clear entry points
Questionnaire content structure Form IA

Questions are grouped into four logical blocks to keep the interaction short and focused:

  • Basic profile (age, gender)
  • Health notes (blood sugar, weight, caffeine sensitivity, cardiovascular)
  • Current scene (workout, office, overtime, craving)
  • Goals (sugar control, hydration/electrolytes, alertness, taste)
Recommendation content structure Result IA

The recommendation screen organises information into:

  • Health snapshot (summary of input)
  • Top picks (highest priority drinks with tags)
  • More options (secondary, still-compatible drinks)
  • Vending mapping (slot highlighting concept)
  • Nutrition detail modal placeholder
Supporting information Meta IA

Supporting content is separated from the core flow so that end-users are not overloaded, but tutors and stakeholders can still inspect the logic:

  • Profile & history: health tags, past sessions, trends
  • Points & challenges: how loyalty links to healthy behaviour
  • About: project background, pain points, role of the prototype
